# JSInside Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Absolute beginners learning JavaScript internals for the first time. They may feel intimidated by "developer" aesthetics — dark terminals, dense docs, jargon-heavy interfaces. The job: make JS internals feel approachable, even delightful. The emotional goal is to replace anxiety with curiosity.

### Brand Personality
**Playful, curious, handmade.** Like a smart friend who draws diagrams in a notebook while explaining something confusing. Not corporate. Not "dev tool." Not another tutorial site.

Three words: **curious · warm · alive**

### Aesthetic Direction
**"Curious Kid's Notebook"** — a bright, cream-toned notebook filled with excited annotations, doodles in the margins, highlighted passages, and hand-drawn diagrams. Imagine a Leuchtturm1917 dot-grid notebook that someone has been filling with JS notes.

- **Theme**: Light mode default. Warm cream paper tone — not clinical white.
- **Background**: Dot-grid pattern (like a Leuchtturm notebook) in warm cream
- **Color palette**:
  - Background: `oklch(0.97 0.015 85)` — warm cream
  - Text: `oklch(0.18 0.025 60)` — warm ink-brown, not pure black
  - Highlight: amber/yellow `oklch(0.85 0.14 85)` — felt-tip highlighter
  - Accent: terracotta `oklch(0.58 0.13 45)` — for CTAs and emphasis
  - Doodle ink: `oklch(0.35 0.06 60)` — warm sepia/brown
  - Secondary: sage green `oklch(0.72 0.08 145)` — for secondary elements
- **NO**: purple gradients, dark mode defaults, neon accents, glassmorphism
- **Reference**: arjun-r.com — narrative-driven scrolling, conversational, human, minimalist yet playful with microinteractions

### Typography
- **Display**: Fraunces (optical-size serif with personality — feels handmade and editorial)
- **Body**: Lora (warm humanist serif — comfortable to read, not clinical)
- **Code**: JetBrains Mono (keep as-is, existing choice)
- Scale: fluid with clamp(), generous line-height (1.7–1.8 for body)

### Design Principles
1. **Notebook-first**: Every element should feel like it belongs in a physical notebook — annotations, doodles, highlighted text, margin notes
2. **Motion earns its place**: Scroll animations reveal content like writing appearing on a page; no animation for decoration alone
3. **Beginners first**: Dense technical content must breathe — generous spacing, progressive disclosure, plain language callouts
4. **Microinteractions over macrointeractions**: Subtle hover jiggle, press-down buttons, highlight sweeps — delight in the details
5. **Asymmetry is intentional**: Left-aligned content with margin space for annotations; break the grid for emphasis
